Question 1:
My dashboard answers the first question by showing a horizontal bar chart that tells the top 10 most sold games by year, including all years up to 2016. If a game appeared on multiple consoles in a certain year, then the sales for each console are summed together. The user could interact with this data by selecting a year of interest in the dropdown, or selecting All Years to see the top 10 of all time.
My dashboard answers the second question by showing a map that tells the most popular genre of game upon hovering over a region. The tooltip shows the name of the region, the most popular genre for that region, and number of copies sold in millions.
My dashboard answers the third question by showing a donut chart that tells the top 10 leading publishers by genre. All the remaining publishers are categorized under “Other.” The user could interact with this data by selecting a genre of interest in the dropdown. The leading publisher for that genre is easily identifiable as the largest portion on the donut graph.

Question 4:
I believe my dashboard is decently accessible. Because I knew I will be dealing with several pie sectors, instead of using a standard pie chart with a separate key map that relies on color, I used a donut chart and labeled data points on the donut sectors directly. I also separated elements with whitespace on the donut chart, so that viewers can more easily distinguish between data. Further, I stayed away from using sliders for users to manipulate the various graphs, as suggested by the betterment article. Instead, I used dropdown filters. Lastly, I stayed away from hover overlays as much as possible (with the exception of the map) so that viewers can more quickly process information. I believe any users who have had experience interacting with any WebApp element would be able to comfortably access this dashboard. Users who are extremely nearsighted might have a hard time reading some of the labels in my dashboard. For this reason, as part of the additional actions, I might make some of my labels larger to make this dashboard more accessible to all audiences without sacrificing the content of the graph.
